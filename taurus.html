Taurus

April 20th - May 20th








Due to the Equinox's precession, the Taurus Zodiac sign does not correspond to the Taurus constellation. It represents the second 30 degrees of the zodiacal circle and continues the Aries sign. Spring and life's beginnings are symbolized by the sign of Aries, while Taurus is a fixed star that carries on what Aries started. Life blossoms to its fullest in the sign of Taurus. The Pleiades and the Hyades, two open clusters made up of the stars of the Taurus constellation, are centered towards the conclusion of the Taurus zodiac sign and the start of the Gemini sign.


Like Aries in antiquity, it was used to denote the Sun's position during the spring equinox in the year 2000 BC. It was connected to it between 5000 and 1700 BC before precession moved our vision to Aries. One of the earliest constellations that was known to exist was Taurus. However, it was not until Babylonian astronomy that it was portrayed as a bull called "The Bull of Heaven" or "The Bull in Front," although it had been seen in cave paintings as early as 15,000 BC. Numerous bull images were influenced by its function in agricultural calendars in prehistoric Sumer, Akkad, Assyria, Babylonia, Egypt, Greece, and Rome.


Because Venus' archetypal traits—receptive, feminine, or yin qualities—dominate Taurus, people are frequently drawn to meditation and other forms of inner awareness. You have a steady, forgiving, and giving spirit at the core of your personality if Taurus is your rising sign, sun sign, or even moon sign. The Taurus is a sustainer with the qualities of being steadfast because it is a fixed sign. Those born under the sign of the Bull are renowned for being excellent at committing themselves to seeing relationships and projects through.


Venus rules Taurus, so those born under this sign are known as the "builders" of the zodiac. They put in a lot of effort to create and improve material wealth and everything that makes life attractive, nourishing, or enjoyable. The main traits of the Taurus are consistency, resourcefulness, patience, and productivity. They avoid conflict and are affable and receptive. Taurus's favor reason and logic over mysticism. They are a fixed earth sign, so you can rely on them to protect and uphold the things you value most, whether they are in your relationships or at work. Taurus residents are dependable and stable in finishing what they start and devoted in maintaining their promises, whereas cardinal signs excel at beginning things and changeable signs thrive at evolving things. Taurus people are patient learners, but because they are a fixed earth sign, they may need some time to assimilate new knowledge or abilities. But that does not imply that they are poor students.


They have a strong attention capacity; thus they almost never forget a lesson. Tauruses are compelled by the beauty of nature and the sensuality of their own bodies since Venus rules their lives. Being outside and spending time walking or working in the dirt makes you feel balanced and rejuvenated, which is why Tauruses adore doing these things. Tauruses love to collect, assemble, and share beautiful, well-made treasures. They are typically creative and skilled at all types of handicrafts. The Taurus' greatest strengths can also be its greatest shortcomings. Although they are calm and patient, this could eventually cause them to become excessively passive, which makes them more prone to becoming bored and unmotivated. Fixed signs, especially earth signs, which might be viewed as being resistant to change and excessively devoted to their methods and attitude, have a tremendous impact. Although they are not particularly vengeful, Taurus's long memory suggests that they will not often forget a mistake, which may make it difficult for others to win back their trust for a while. They tend to respect their values a lot more that way, though, so this could also be seen as a strength.
