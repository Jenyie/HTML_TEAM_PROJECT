Gemini

May 21st - June 21st








The constellation of Gemini is not where Gemini is. According to other signs of the zodiac, it follows Taurus and is at 30 degrees in the third zodiac sign. Gemini is a mutable sign that governs the period after Taurus spring has ended and before Earthly life is about to change. With the beginning of Cancer, spring is ending, and summer is getting ready. One of the constellations that truly bears the name "Gemini" is the word "twins," from the Latin "Gemini," which signifies. Over 300 years ago, in his Meteorologica, Aristotle became the first to describe the zodiac sign of Gemini.


According to Aristotle, he spoke of seeing Jupiter in conjunction with a star that was occulting in Gemini. In Babylonian astrology, these stars were referred to as the great twins and minor gods Meshlamtaea (The One who has ascended from the Underworld) and Lugalirra (The Mighty King). Between Taurus in the west and Cancer in the east is this zodiac. The zodiac is distinctive in that actual stars, not just constellations, are used to mark the twins' heads. The two brightest stars in Gemini are Castor and Pollux; the former is an orange-hued giant star, and the latter is a six-star system that looks to the unaided eye to be a blue-white star. According to its archetypal characteristics, which are influenced by its active, male, or yang aspects, Gemini's personality is distinguished by its assertive involvement with the outer world.


People who were born under the Twins as their rising, sun, or moon sign possess a curious vitality in their core personalities, much like quick-thinking Mercury. Gemini is a fluid sign that is known for bringing about change, thus those who have a lot of Gemini in their horoscopes may naturally have an interest in adapting, learning new things, and networking to open new avenues for awareness. The twins' greatest strengths are their intelligence and effective, lucid communication. They are affected by Mercury's rapid and analytical manner, which makes them rarely bored and able to launch themselves headlong into life with an insatiable thirst for information.


The Twins' dualistic symbolism suggests that the Gemini native has a special ability to "shape-shift." They can easily acclimate to any culture or tongue and take on the traditions of regional customs and languages. Gemini is a fun-loving, gregarious, and lighthearted sign for persons born under it. They are excellent at making connections between people and the right people so that assets, skills, and knowledge can be successfully disseminated in their communities. A Gemini may assess you quickly and have a keen sense of which buttons to press. Some of Gemini's best traits can also expose blind spots or vulnerabilities. They need to learn how to absorb, integrate, and use this knowledge in a useful way because they might rely too heavily on encyclopedic knowledge. They may have a short attention span and prefer to dance on the shiny surface of discovery due to their sharp, inquisitive minds rather than fully committing themselves to learning a subject thoroughly. A Gemini is a person with lots of energy who finds flexibility to be enjoyable.
