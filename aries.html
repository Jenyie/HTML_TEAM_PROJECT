The Aries zodiac sign is distinct from the actual Aries constellation. It shows the latitude the Sun will cover in about 30.4 days (about 4 and a half weeks), beginning at the vernal equinox, the beginning of spring. Position 0Â° on the zodiac wheel belongs to it. When the zodiac system was developed in Babylonia approximately 2,500 years ago, during "The Age of Aries," the beginning of the sign of Aries coincided with the projection of the constellation of Aries, hence the name of the sign. The projection of the constellation shifted because of precession, but the sign of Aries remained where it was on the first day of spring. Like all other constellations, Aries was formed in the night sky to tell a story. story. It was given that name in honor of the flying ram, a significant figure in an old Greek story. In some cultures, the constellation of Aries does not signify a ram. For instance, the Marshall Islands believe the constellation to represent a porpoise, while the Chinese believe it to be two pairs of inspectors.
